Project Brief

An exquisite architecture with Luxury, Comfort and Class.

Our task was to build or rebrand Archmove’s website that showed off their exquisite collection of architecture in a way that not only captivates the consumer but draws them in. It was a big one!

We were asked to help re-establish an online which was lost. We led the design work, well as helped them find a new development that could meet their ongoing needs as well as a new design approach. Eventually, our relationship developed into us becoming brand guardians.

For one, we can’t quite say this is a case study because it’ll be more storyish, or reiteration of best practices and methods applied throughout our development process. Welcome to our case study!

Our task was set. It’s our turn to accomplish it and overdo just a little.

UX/UI

Every part and process of the User Experience has to feel organic. Our philosophy is that whenever the user can barely notice or tell interactive changes or phases between interactions, then our job is half done. I can’t quite say this is a case study because it’ll be more story- ish, but hey! Welcome to my first case study! So to start with, the design brief looked something like “Design a landing page for your friend’s restaurant business. The entire page should be done solely with typography. No vector illustrations of food or persons is allowed.” That was new! In all my few) months of design, I had always used images to draw the user’s eye and evoke the emotions I wanted to. Now I had to do it all with typography! A few things became immediately clear:

Visual identity

Archmove is a major construction business based in Ohio, USA. Their main business was quality architecture and building comfortable homes. So I had to make sure that this message came across on their website. Their visual identity has to speak to trust, quality and value. And there were no better colors than these. Blue signifies loyalty and trust, that reddish-orange signals bright quality, toned down against the dark blue which embeds the quality of true value.

Typography

Branding

A company’s brand or business believe it or not has a hiuge role to play in its successes. Great Brands are memorable, simple and clear. This was our motivation, it had to go down in the records That every aspect of creating a cohesive brand identity had to glue together or else, it may result In something we like to call ‘Disjointed Brand’ — where the brand speaks one thing different from what it actually means or intends to mean. Tis clash of identity and purpose is crucial. Our team tackles by having an in-depth study into how best their brand could be elevated, what’s missing and what has to go. More often the not, we find ourselves subtracting elements than adding elements to an already well-designed narrative.

Tackling mobile was also a huge part of the task we had, something the client felt very strongly about. Again, our modular approach really proved it’s worth as we able to more tightly control the UX for mobile.
